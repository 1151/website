Officer Commanding

Flight Lieutenant Yarrow is a commissioned officer in the Royal Air Force Volunteer Reserve (Training Branch) and commands the Squadron. Nigel took over in 1997 after serving at the unit for over ten years. Nigel is one of the longest serving Commissioned Officers in the RAFVR(T) with over 42 years service to the ATC. Nigel started out as a cadet at 1027 (Jarrow) Squadron where he reached the rank of Cadet Warrant Officer. After a long career around the Wing in post at Wideopen and Gateshead Squadron’s Nigel was asked to assist at Wallsend Squadron. Flt Lt Yarrow takes an keen interest in Fieldcraft training and is part of the North Regions Fieldcraft Training Team as well as being a qualified First Aid Instructor and holding a BELA qualification for outdoor activities.

Deputy Officer Commanding

Flight Lieutenant Handalage was Commissioned in 2003 and joined 1151 in August 2015 following service in several other Squadrons including fulfilling the role of Squadron Officer at 207 (Cranfield) and OC at 1338 (Seaham). Chris is takes a leading role in the Squadron's shooting and Duke of Edinburgh activities.

Squadron Adult NCO's

1151 currently doesn't have any Adult Non-Commissioned Officers on squadron, but recruitment, development and training is an on-going process. Cadets can progress through the ranks to become an Adult NCO's or new adult volunteers aged over 20 can join the ATC as a Civillian Instructor and then if they wish, they can progress into the role.

Civillian Insructors

The majority of the staff on Squadron consists of Civillian Instructors (CIs). CIs help organise and run a variety of activities, whether these are conducted on or off the Squadron. The CIs at 1151 (Wallsend) are vital in the nightly running of the Squadron and delivery of training to the cadets.

Flying

As an Air Cadet you'll have many opportunities to get up in the air. Whether it's as a part of an Air Experience Flight or being flown in a military aircraft on a camp, all cadets will have the chance to take to the air, should they choose to do so. You will also have the option to take part in the gliding scholarship or flying scholarship programmes, allowing you complete a solo flight.

Shooting

Shooting offers you the chance to be trained on a number of rifles. Starting with the No.8 Rifle working up to the L98A2 - a variant of the current standard issue service rifle - with the possibility of using the L81A2 competition rifle. All of these allow you, as a cadet, to obtain marksmanship awards with the opportunity to compete in the national Cadet 100 event and Skill At Arms.

Gliding

As well as various opportunities to go flying in all sorts of powered aircraft, you will also be given the option to have a go in a gilder.
At the age of 16, you can apply to do your gliding scholarship and may even fly solo, before you can drive a car!

Duke of Edinburgh Award

The Duke of Edinburgh Award is a nationally recognised scheme that focuses on developing a number of attributes that are split into 4 key areas:
Expedition
Volunteering
Skill
Physical Activity
This can be completed on squadron with help available from the resident staff.

Fieldcraft

Many people associate cadets with running around on exercise, however this is not necessarily true. Most of the skill you will learn will be in the field but there is some theory behind this. The fieldcraft training you will recieve will allow you to camoflauge yourself to your environment, move tactically and work as a team, as well as teaching you the skills to maintain yourself and your equiptment.

First Aid

As a cadet you'll also be given the opportunity to take part in the youth fist-aider course. Which is accredited by St. Johns Ambulance, giving you a widely recognised workplace qualification, and the skills that could safe a life.
The best part is... it’s free!
